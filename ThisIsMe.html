Book List

The 48 Laws Of Power
- The 48 Laws Of Power draws on many of history's most famous power quarrels to show you what power looks like, how you can get it, what to do to defend yourself against the power of others and, most importantly, how to use it well and keep it.
Atomic Habits
- Atomic Habits is the definitive guide to breaking bad behaviors and adopting good ones in four steps, showing you how small, incremental, everyday routines compound into massive, positive change over time.
The 4-Hour Workweek
- The 4-Hour Workweek is the step-by-step blueprint to free yourself from the shackles of a corporate job, create a business to fund the lifestyle of your dreams, and live life like a millionaire, without actually having to be one.
The Magic of Thinking Big
- The Magic of Thinking Big gives you several starting points to develop and strengthen the most important trait of successful people: believing in yourself.
The Millionaire Fastlane
- The Millionaire Fastlane points out what's wrong with the old get a degree, get a job, work hard, retire rich model, defines wealth in a new way, and shows you the path to retiring young.
Hard Times Creates Strong Men
- Hard Times Creates Strong Men examines what it means to be a man in the modern world. This book examines what worked and what doesn't work based on proven history instead of feelings.

Values

Doing The Hard Work Especially When You Don't Feel Like It
- Doing hard work even when it doesn't feel like it matters can lead to personal growth and a sense of accomplishment. It also sets a good work ethic and can lead to future opportunities. Additionally, putting effort into tasks can improve skills and build discipline.
Belief And Mindset
- Belief is important for mindset because it shapes one's thoughts, attitudes, and perceptions. A strong belief system helps to maintain a positive and optimistic outlook, foster resilience, and provide direction and motivation in life. It can also influence decision-making, behavior, and overall mental well-being.
Discomfort
- Discomfort makes someone grow because it pushes them out of their comfort zone and challenges their limitations. It forces them to face new experiences and learn new skills, leading to personal growth and development. Overcoming discomfort also builds confidence and resilience, preparing one to handle difficult situations in the future. It can also lead to new perspectives and insights, as well as the development of new strengths and abilities.
